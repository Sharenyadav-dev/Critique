Reading Frontier

Exploring how nations balance publishing output and reading engagement.
Each visualization draws on national-level data: titles published, books read per person, and annual reading time.

Insights

  • Australia: 10 books/person, 217 hrs/year (~21.7 hrs/book).
  • Peers: US, UK, India & NZ show higher reading intensity per output.
  • Reading productivity frontier: Nations in the upper-right maximize both production & participation.
  • Time intensity: Larger bubbles = more hours spent reading annually.

Insights

  • High Output · High Reading: US & UK dominate this space — strong publishing sectors + high readership.
  • High Output · Low Reading: Economically large but less reader-engaged nations.
  • Low Output · High Reading: Smaller markets with vibrant reading cultures (Nordic, Eastern Europe).
  • Australia: Positioned mid-range — solid reading levels but moderate output relative to peers.

Sources: average-books-read-per-year-by-country-2025.csv; books-published-per-year-by-country-2025.csv; global_reading_time_map.csv.
